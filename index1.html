    How To Visit Chinese Cupid



    supervisor_ Yu Hsuan, Liu
    Project Manager_ Yi Ming, He
    Graphic Design_ Yen Heng, Chang
    Copy Writer_ Yi Ming, He
    Event Planner_ Yu Hsuan, Liu / Hong Jun, Lin


    Background

    Xia-Hai City God Temple is well known of it’s stunnig historic building. But, there is one more reason that makes this temple being so popular. It is Yue-Lao’s( Love God in Chinese culture) matchmaking skills, which make many tourists from all over the world come here to pray for their luck in love.

    Temple plays an important role in Taiwanese culture, but there are many details you have to notice when you want to visit the Gods, such as route and taboo. It could be more profound or feel easier for the tourists if they know the steps through the whole visiting procedure before they come here. So, we decide to design a flow chart of how to visit Chinese Cupid.



    Plate 1. Xia-Hai City God Temple


    Concept

    We went to visit the temple and went through the whole process. As a result, we found out that the route is actually quite complicated and there are 2 to 3 things you have to carry during the process, too.

    After having some discussions with the team. I decided to illustrate the temple to a exploded drawing for two reason. First, to make the image look more conformable and more soothing in general, since we don’t want put the information to close in a specific area. Secondly, divide the processes in different parts( prepare、worship、ending) is easier for readers to receive the information.



    Infographic

    While designing this project, I use the elements which have meanings in Chinese culture. For instances, the following path is a “red line”, which ties the couples together in the original story of Yue-Lao; the notification and the QA sections’( down below) labels are “fortune sticks”, which are used to get the information from gods. And there are still many eastern eggs related to Chinese culture in this infographic.




    Event & Poster

    After we finished the infographic, it was also close to Valentine day. So, we planned a activity with the temple together. After they finished the activity, they can get a poster we made. To make the poster being more interesting and utilize the space more effectively, I also designed a pair of spring couplets on it. They can tear the spring couplets down and stick them up, which means praying for good luck in Chinese culture.

    Plate 4. the photo we took after the event

    What happened to Taiwain's 815 power outage



    Content cooperation and guideline_ PanSci
    supervisor_ Yu Hsuan, Liu
    Project Manager_ Wei Jun, Chang
    Graphic Design_ Yen Heng, Chang
    Copy Writer_ Wei Jun, Chang
    Motion Design_ Ren Chih, Yeh


    Background

    An unexpected power outrage stroke Taiwan at 16:51 in 15 August 2017, approximately 6 million users were influenced by it. And the problem were solved at 21:40. People were asking that is Taiwan lack of electricity and what did Taiwan Power Company actually do during the power outrage?

    We decide to make a motion graphic to illustrate what action did TPC take while the power outrage was happening. We hope the readers can understand by watching our video. Though TPC released their own official report, but is very hard to absorb by people who is not familiar with this field.



    Plate 1. TPC's report


    Concept

    There is monitoring screens with many kinds of statistics monitored by the staff of TPC, so they can make decision immediately when there’s any problem pops up. We decide to design an interface that mimic the TPC’s monitoring screen, hoping the readers can get into the scenario and understand what’s going on easier.



    Plate 2. the process of making the video


    Infographic

    After watching the video, people might still wonder that what kind of power planet is better for Taiwan. If there is a power outrage happen again in the future, which kind of power planet can generate the power faster and support the whole power supply system if some of the planets broken?

    So, we also made a infographic to show the readers how fast does each kinds of power planets generate the power.



    Plate 3. the process and of making the infographic

    Dr. Micro



    co-designer_ Hao，Zheng / Sheng Yi, Chen
    role_ concept design / graphic design / app design


    Background

    Taiwan is suffered from different diseases in different seasons, such as dengue fever in the summer and flu in the winter.

    The typical incubation period for influenza is 1 to 4 days. Most people may be able to infect others beginning 1 day before symptoms develop and up to 5 to 7 days after becoming sick. Some people, especially young children and people with weakened immune systems, might be able to infect others for an even longer time. But, most of the people won’t go to a doctor when they get a flu at the beginning, and that will increase their death rate, moreover, they will infect others in the same time.To learn how to prevent the epidemic expansion effectively, it’s crucial to have enough data to analyze.

    Concept

    According to an online reservation in Taiwan in 2016, 55.4% of people wore the mask when there were air quality or flu issue been reported. So, we think mask is a good way to gain the data, since it is common for people wearing them to prevent from the bad air quality and disease in eastern countries. We hoped to collect the data through saliva testing ( a diagnostic technique based on a chemical reaction called antigen-antibody agglutination, pregnancy test is also based on this reaction) from their mask.



    Plate 1. a mask equipe with a testing function
    Plate 2. the antigen-antibody agglutination


    Chip design

    We design a chip which contains two layers, the upper layer with QR code and the under layer with the testing papers. There are holes on the QR code, and testing papers under it will make the code shows up different patterns because of the different results of the them. And the users need to scan the code to know the result. At the same time, the data is uploaded simultaneously.



    Plate 3. layers of the chip, and their functions


    Plate 4. the chip
    Plate 5. infection rate in different areas (based on users data)


    App design

    After users scan the chip, the datas will be uploaded simultaneously, so we can analyze the infection rate of each area. Meanwhile, government can make a more appropriate decision on disease control. In order to reward the user to upload the data, Dr. Micro offer discounts of cooperate companies to them.



    Plate 6. flow map of the app


    Operation

    Dr.Micro is a diseases detection system based on Big Data, effectively solving infection problems and building a network for diseases-prevention analysis. We hope all kind of masks equipped with the system of Dr. Micro. With government’s support ( includes operating or importing the diseases' test papers), we hope this system can go further.

    Blowin Sumo



    material_ wood / plastic tube / styrofoam ball


    Background

    Taiwan has already been an aging society since 1993. According to Ministry of Interior of Taiwan, the proportion of elderly people of Taiwan ranks number 3 in Asia, behind Japan and South Korea. Therefore, it becomes more and more important to develop and improve the Long-term care and Seniors products.

    It is very important to maintain cardiovascular function in older age, it will decrease the chance to get diseases and improve other functions in our body. There is a medical device called incentive spirometer( it is often recommended by doctors to their elder patients after heart related surgeries), which is used to help people improve their lungs’ function. And I think that device is very interesting, but it could be better if it is more human and emotional. Therefore, I wanted to redesign it.


    Plate 1. incentive spirometer


    Concept

    Since elders could resist to new things rather than normal people, so it is crucial to think deeply about mental aspect. Firstly, I decided to make it a 2 players interactive device, which meant the elders can play it with their partner or their grandchildren. We all know that elders could be happier when being accompany. Secondly, I wanted the device having Chinese or Japanese culture element, because Taiwan is a country profoundly influenced by these two countries( Taiwan had been ruled by Japan from 1895A.D. to 1945A.D.).

    According to previous reasons I mentioned, I decided to make the device as a Sumo Battle( a traditional Japanese Wrestling). There are two sides of the device, each side have a figure. Each figure is driven by each user, the user have to blow to make the figure move forward to push against the opponent, the one who makes their opponent’s figure touch to the border win.



    Plate 2. Japanese sumo


    Mechanism and Functions

    There are two individual tubes connect to plastic pipes with styrofoam balls in it( hygienic purpose), and the balls connect to the figures. Therefore, when you blow to the tube, the figure will go forward.

    PostPET



    material_ plastic bottle / aluminum / wood， etc
    co-designer_ Hsin Ming，Peng
    role_ VI / experiment / design


    Background

    There aren’t any incinerator working in offshore islands in Taiwan now.Therefore, they can only ship trash to Taiwan. Since the growing number of tourists, the problem shows up as well.

    In oder to figure out which kind of trash is the most delicate issue on the island, we send the questionnaires, and interview the environment volunteerson on the island. Consider to the quantity and volume, we find out that pastic bottles could be the biggest trouble.



    Plate 1. the growing amount of trash is positive correlation with the number of tourists in Siao Liouciou



    Plate 2. the graph of how many trash did a person produce in a trip in Siao Liouciou


    Concept

    We want the tourists bring back their own trash. Firstly, we need to make them rethink the issue. Secondly, we need to increase their willing to bring back the trash. So, we decide to make their own trash transform to a postcard, which means the trash leave the island also the idea spreads out. And the trash they will transform is the "plastic bottles they used".

    In order to reduce the volume and processing timing, we chose to hot pressing the bottles.

    We want make the tourists follow the procedure in a certain place, we decided to make a little station that can do these steps easily. We hope all the tourists can be happy and honor to help the insland .



    Self-service DIY Station

    Following the steps from the right to the left, the tourist will finish their postcard in approximatly 15 minutes, we also Laser engraving the method of every steps on the table to guide them.

    Plate 3. details


    Plate 4. the steps of making PostPET


    PostPET

    Tourists can recognize their own postcard, because it will show up the colors of the bottles they bring.

    Food’n Food



    material_ green bean noodle / grape juice / cola (you can eat them all)
    co-designer_ Wei Qian，Hong
    role_ experiment


    Background

    Rice、noodle are the major carbohydrate we consume in Asia. We noticed that some of the carbohydrate is transparent in some extent, and they became hard when they being anhydrous. And there is a kind of traditional Chinese noodle that is more transparent than any other carbohydrate, called “green bean noodle”.

    We also find out green bean noodle can be easily colored by some drink, which contain pigment, like juice and cola, etc. Furthermore, we found out that it will melt when it cook with cola. Since those attributes make green bean noodle so special and interesting, we decide to display it by reshaping them in different form.

    We felt like it would be interesting if it was a container of foods, which meant the foods are in the foods, just like ice cream corn.



    Plate 1. rice show up transparent in some extent with lights
    Plate 2. green rice noodle can be easily reshaped


    Procedure

    We cooked them first to soften them. That process make them able to being reshaped by putting them in container we made. We made a bowl with holes with 3D printing, make sure that the noddle can evaporate faster, then we put them in to the oven with the same reason, then, the noodle will be dry and shaped.

    Plate 3. different layers

    Plate 4. the steps of reshape noodle by putting them in to the molds


    Exhibit

    We decide to exhibit them on the light box, in order to make the color and the texture be more obvious.
